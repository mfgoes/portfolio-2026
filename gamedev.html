The Challenge: Balancing Vision with Constraints

Solo project managing a real-time strategy game. The core challenge: delivering a compelling experience within aggressive timeline and resource constraints.

This project mirrors real-world product management: systems work technically, but require strategic iteration to achieve user engagement. Building something functional is different from building something people want to use.

Product Strategy: Replayability Over Feature Bloat

After December's development sprint, a critical insight emerged: the game works but playthroughs feel identical. Players follow quest instructions linearly without meaningful decisions.

Core Insight

Make existing systems interesting before adding new ones

Shifted from feature addition to variance creation

Priority Pivot

Variance in experience beats system complexity

Deferred ice resources, vehicles, and fire hazards

Portfolio Focus

Target: Playable demo by January 30, 2025

Constrained scope to maximize learning showcase

Structured Roadmap Planning

Applied PM frameworks to solo game development: weekly sprints with measurable success metrics

Week 1: Onboarding

Goal: Reduce early drop-off

Address 80% Day 3 survival rate through softer difficulty curve and variance.

Metric: Two playthroughs feel different

Week 2: Exploration

Goal: Core engagement mechanic

Force strategic decision-making before committing to layout choices.

Metric: Players explore before building

Week 3: Player Agency

Goal: Strategic choices

Transform linear flow into branching paths based on player situation.

Metric: 50%+ variance in paths

Week 4: Portfolio Ready

Goal: Showcase-ready state

Polish and capture materials for portfolio presentation.

Deliverable: Demo video + case study

Leveraging AI for Accelerated Execution

Managing both product strategy and technical execution required force multipliers. Integrated Claude Code to maintain velocity while staying focused on strategic decisions.

This approach mirrors modern product development: humans define strategy and validate outcomes, while AI tools accelerate implementation. The result is faster iteration cycles without sacrificing product vision.

Workflow
  1. Define: Requirements and success criteria
  2. Collaborate: AI-assisted implementation
  3. Validate: Measure against metrics
  4. Iterate: Refine based on user behavior

Scope Management: What Got Deferred and Why

Critical PM skill: knowing what not to build. The January roadmap explicitly deferred features to Q2 based on impact vs. complexity analysis.

Deferred Features

Why: Complexity without addressing core engagement

New resource systems, vehicles, and hazards pushed to Q2. Focus on making existing mechanics compelling first.

Impact vs. Effort

Decision Framework: High-impact, low-complexity first

Prioritized randomization and player choice over new systems requiring extensive testing.
